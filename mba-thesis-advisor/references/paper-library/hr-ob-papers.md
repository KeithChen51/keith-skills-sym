### 理论：赫茨伯格双因素理论 (Herzberg's Two-Factor Theory)

- id: "herzberg-1959-book"
  role: "origin"
  title_en: "The Motivation to Work"
  authors: ["Frederick Herzberg", "Bernard Mausner", "Barbara Bloch Snyderman"]
  year: 1959
  source: "John Wiley & Sons"
  contribution: "This book is the foundational text for Herzberg's Two-Factor Theory, also known as the Motivation-Hygiene Theory. It introduces the core concepts of 'motivators' (factors that lead to satisfaction) and 'hygiene factors' (factors that prevent dissatisfaction), based on interviews with 200 engineers and accountants. The book argues that satisfaction and dissatisfaction are not on a single continuum, but are influenced by different sets of factors."
  cite_gbt7714: "[1]HERZBERG F, MAUSNER B, SNYDERMAN B B. The motivation to work[M]. New York: John Wiley & Sons, 1959."

  authors: ["Frederick Herzberg"]
  year: 1968
  source: "Harvard Business Review"
  contribution: "This article is one of the most influential articles in the history of Harvard Business Review. It popularized Herzberg's Two-Factor Theory and introduced the concept of 'job enrichment'. Herzberg argues that to truly motivate employees, managers should focus on improving the content of the job itself, rather than relying on 'hygiene' factors like salary and benefits."
  cite_gbt7714: "[1]HERZBERG F. One more time: how do you motivate your employees?[J]. Harvard business review, 1968, 46(1): 53-62."
